Nutrition

General Information for Typical Healthy Diets

  1. 1. Mediterranean diet: Rich in vegetables, fruits, nuts, whole grains, lean proteins and healthy fats like olive oil. It has been linked to reduced risk of cancer and heart disease.
  2. 2. DASH diet: Focuses on fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins and low-fat dairy. It has been linked to lower risk of heart disease, stroke, and cancer.
  3. 3. Plant-based diet: High in fruits, vegetables, legumes, whole grains, nuts and seeds. It has been linked to lower risk of heart disease, type 2 diabetes, and cancer.
  4. 4. Low-fat diet: Emphasizes fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins. It has been linked to lower risk of heart disease and certain types of cancer.

Specific Diet Needs of Cancer Patients

  1. 1. High-calorie and high-protein diet: Helps to maintain energy levels and support healing. Recommended for patients undergoing chemotherapy.
  2. 2. Low-fiber diet: Helps to reduce bowel movements and alleviate diarrhea. Recommended for patients experiencing bowel issues during chemotherapy.
  3. 3. Low-sugar diet: Helps to maintain a healthy weight and reduce inflammation. Recommended for patients with high blood sugar levels.
  4. 4. High-antioxidant diet: Helps to reduce inflammation and support the immune system. Recommended for patients undergoing radiation therapy.

Specific Diet Needs for Cancer Patients

  • 1 Protein: Protein is important for maintaining muscle mass and strength during cancer treatment. Good sources of protein include lean meats, poultry, fish, eggs, beans, and tofu.
  • 2 Fruits and Vegetables: Fruits and vegetables are packed with vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants that can help support the immune system during cancer treatment. Aim for a variety of colors and types, and try to eat at least five servings per day.
  • 3 Healthy Fats: Healthy fats, such as those found in nuts, seeds, avocados, and fatty fish, can help reduce inflammation and support heart health.
  • 4 Whole Grains: Whole grains are a good source of fiber, which can help regulate digestion and support a healthy gut microbiome. Examples of whole grains include brown rice, quinoa, and whole wheat bread.
  • 5 Fluids: It's important to stay hydrated during cancer treatment, especially if you experience side effects like vomiting or diarrhea. Aim for at least eight glasses of water per day, and consider drinking other fluids like herbal tea or coconut water.

    Baked Cod with Tomato and Olive Salad

    Ingredients:

    • 4 cod fillets
    • 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
    • 1/2 cup pitted kalamata olives, chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
    • 2 tablespoons olive oil
    • 1 lemon, sliced
    • Salt and pepper, to taste

    Directions:

    1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees F.
    2. Season the cod fillets with salt and pepper, and place them in a baking dish.
    3. In a bowl, combine the cherry tomatoes, olives, garlic, parsley, and olive oil. Season with salt and pepper, and toss to combine.
    4. Spoon the tomato and olive mixture over the cod fillets, and place lemon slices on top.
    5. Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork.

  • Specific Diet Needs for Cancer Patients

    1. High-Protein Foods

      High-protein foods such as lean meats, fish, eggs, dairy, and legumes are essential for maintaining and repairing tissues damaged by cancer treatments.

      • Grilled Lemon Herb Mediterranean Chicken Salad
      • Southwestern Chicken Soup
      • Black Bean and Quinoa Salad

    2. Iron-Rich Foods

      Cancer treatments can lower red blood cell counts, which can lead to anemia. Iron-rich foods such as red meat, poultry, fish, beans, and leafy green vegetables can help combat anemia.

      • Beef and Broccoli Stir-Fry
      • Spinach and Feta Stuffed Salmon
      • Lentil and Vegetable Curry

    3. Fiber-Rich Foods

      Fiber-rich foods such as fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and legumes can help prevent constipation, a common side effect of cancer treatments.

      • Grilled Vegetable Skewers
      • Sweet Potato and Black Bean Chili
      • Mixed Berry and Yogurt Parfait
